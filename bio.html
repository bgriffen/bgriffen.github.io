Brief Bio

Hi. I use technology to understand the following:
  • complex biological systems
  • the origin of our universe
  • the development of human ideas
  • how better to educate the next generation

You can find a little bit about each of these on parts of this website. On a more personal level, here is a bit more about me.

First Decade

I was born in Hong Kong and then moved through various cities in Australia, finally ending up in Bargara, Queensland. I played a boat load of sports and dabbled with a musical side but ultimately set on science and mathematics as my main pursuit.

Second Decade

I moved to Brisbane and studied physics, mathematics and philosophy for my undergraduate degree at the University Of Queensland followed by a Masters and PhD in computational astrophysics. Much of my time was spent in Brighton (UK) and Heidelberg (Germany).

Third Decade

I moved to Cambridge, Massachusetts (USA) to continue my astrophysics research as a postdoctoral fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Whilst at MIT I designed and developed The Caterpillar Project, the largest number of high-resolution Milky Way simulations achieved thus far and contributed to the processing pipeline of The Illustris Project which is still one of the most complex astrophysical simulations of our universe done to date.

Now

I've taken a hiatus from formal astrophysics research and moved to San Francisco, California. With two others I co-founded Scaled Biolabs, a company focused on accelerating the advent of the next generation of cell and gene therapies. I recently completed my time in the biotech accelerator Indie Bio.

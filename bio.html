Brief Bio

First Decade

Born in Hong Kong and then moved through various cities in Australia, finally ending up in Bargara, Queensland. I played a boat load of sports and dabbled with a musical side but ultimately converged on science as my main pursuit.

Second Decade

Moved to Brisbane and studied physics, mathematics and philosophy for my undergraduate degree at the University Of Queensland followed by a Masters and PhD in computational astrophysics. Half of my time was spent in Brighton (UK) and Heidelberg (Germany).

Third Decade

Moved to Cambridge, Massachusetts (USA) to continue my astrophysics research as a postdoctoral fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Whilst at MIT I designed and developed The Caterpillar Project, the largest number of high-resolution Milky Way simulations achieved thus far and contributed to the processing pipeline of The Illustris Project which is still one of the most complex astrophysical simulations of our universe done to date.

Now

I've taken a hiatus from formal astrophysics research and moved to San Francisco, California. With two others I co-founded Scaled Biolabs, a company focused on accelerating the advent of the next generation of cell and gene therapies. I am currently immersed in the biotech accelerator Indie Bio.
